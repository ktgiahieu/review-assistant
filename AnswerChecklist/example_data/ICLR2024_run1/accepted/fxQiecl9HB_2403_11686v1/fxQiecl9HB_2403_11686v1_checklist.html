Crystalformer: Infinitely Connected Attention for Periodic Structure Encoding

Paper Link: https://openreview.net/forum?id=fxQiecl9HB

Formatted Abstract

Background and Objectives: Crystal structures are infinite periodic arrangements of atoms; predicting their physical properties without expensive simulations is critical for accelerated material discovery. Transformer models have excelled on finite molecular graphs, but naively extending fully-connected attention to infinite periodic systems appears intractable.

Material and Methods: The authors reinterpret fully-connected attention under periodic boundary conditions as a tractable "neural potential summation" with Gaussian distance-decay. They derive closed-form pseudo-finite periodic attention, introduce periodic spatial/edge encodings, and build Crystalformer—a normalization-free, four-block Transformer that pools atom states for property regression. Training details and adaptive truncation for summations are provided; code is released.

Results: On nine regression tasks from the Materials Project and JARVIS-DFT datasets, Crystalformer achieves lower mean absolute errors than prior GNNs, Transformer variants, and physics-informed PotNet while using only 29 % of Matformer’s parameters and yielding fastest inference. Ablations show the necessity of value position encodings and efficacy of reciprocal-space extensions.

Conclusion: Crystalformer establishes an efficient, physically-motivated Transformer framework for periodic materials, outperforming state-of-the-art baselines with fewer parameters and offering a unified view of infinite potential summations as attention. Extensions to reciprocal space and higher-order/geometric information are discussed as future work.
