CRAFT: Customizing LLMs by Creating and Retrieving from Specialized Toolsets

Paper Link: https://openreview.net/forum?id=G0vdDSt9XM

Formatted Abstract

Background and Objectives: Existing LLM-tool integration mainly relies on fixed, general-purpose APIs, limiting adaptability to specialized tasks. The authors aim to enable flexible task-specific augmentation.

Material and Methods: They propose CRAFT, a framework that automatically creates a toolset from training or instruction data by prompting GPT-4 to generate, abstract, verify and deduplicate code snippets, and retrieves relevant tools at inference with a multi-view similarity scheme.

Results: On vision-language, tabular and math reasoning benchmarks, CRAFT with GPT-3.5-Turbo yields up to 45.4 % soft-accuracy on GQA and large gains over baselines; ablations show each component and larger toolsets/backbones further improve performance.

Conclusion: CRAFT offers a training-free, plug-and-play way to specialize LLMs, releasing validated, reusable tools and demonstrating scalability, reliability and extensibility.
